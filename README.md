Solar Powered Wireless Charging System for Electric Vehicles
Project Overview

The Solar Powered Wireless Charging System for Electric Vehicles (EVs) is an innovative prototype designed to provide a clean, efficient, and contactless EV charging solution using renewable solar energy. The project integrates solar power generation, battery storage, wireless power transfer, and embedded control systems to demonstrate the future of sustainable EV infrastructure.

The system uses inductive power transfer (IPT) technology, where electrical energy is transferred wirelessly through coupled coils operating at resonant frequency. Solar energy generated through photovoltaic panels is stored in a rechargeable battery and later used for wireless charging of the EV load.

Problem Statement

The rapid growth of electric vehicles has increased the demand for efficient and eco-friendly charging systems. Conventional wired charging methods depend on physical connectors and grid electricity, leading to issues such as:

Cable wear and maintenance
Risk of electric shock
User inconvenience
Dependency on non-renewable grid power

This project addresses these challenges by implementing a solar-powered wireless charging system that enables safe and contactless charging using renewable energy.

Proposed Solution

The proposed system combines:

Solar Energy Generation

Arduino-Based Monitoring and Control

The system wirelessly transfers electrical energy from the transmitter coil to the receiver coil using electromagnetic induction, eliminating the need for physical charging connectors.

Working Principle
The solar panel converts sunlight into electrical energy.
The generated energy is stored in a rechargeable battery.
The stored DC power is supplied to a high-frequency switching circuit.
The switching circuit energizes the transmitter coil.
The transmitter coil generates an alternating magnetic field.
The receiver coil captures the magnetic field through electromagnetic induction.
The received electrical energy is rectified and supplied to the load wirelessly.
The Arduino Nano monitors system voltage and charging conditions.
The LCD display shows system parameters and charging status.
A relay module and buzzer indication system provide control and safety functions.
